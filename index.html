About me ☕

Welcome to my website 👋! I recently graduated in Spring 2023 from Cornell University with a Bachelor of Science, where I double majored in Computer Science & Electrical and Computer Engineering.
I am a Full Stack Engineering Intern at Hewlett Packard Enterprise, currently based in Durham, NC. Additionally, I am a Cornell Undergraduate Research Assistant in the Capra Research Group, working remotely under the guidance of Adrian Sampson. My research focuses on Calyx, a compiler infrastructure for languages targeting hardware accelerators. I work on optimizing and transforming programs into synthesizable hardware designs, utilizing Calyx's control language to simplify high-level semantics encoding. Apart from my research, I was actively involved in teaching and mentoring. I serve as a Teaching Assistant for Analysis of Algorithms (CS 4820); Discrete Structures (CS 2800); Circuits for Engineers (ECE 2100) and Linear Algebra (MATH 2940) at Cornell University.
I have gained practical experience through internships as a Software Engineer Intern at JP Morgan Chase and as a Data Science Intern at AXA Equitable, where I worked on both frontend and backend development. During these internships, I worked on developing and analyzing data sets, implementing systems for accurate data visualization, and developing statistical models and algorithms for forecasting customer behaviors.
I'm also a member of IEEE-Eta Kappa Nu, the International Honor society of Electrical and Computer Engineering Cornell Chapter, and Tau Beta Pi, the National Engineering Honor Society Cornell Chapter.
With a strong technical skill set including languages such as Java, Python, and C/C++, and proficiency in various developer tools and libraries, such as Azure and AWS, I am well-equipped to tackle diverse projects and challenges.
